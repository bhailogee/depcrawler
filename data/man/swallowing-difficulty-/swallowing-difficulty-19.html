Swallowing Difficulty

Good -- being young and being a non-smoker makes cancer very unlikely. We can consider less dangerous causes of swallowing difficultly.

Injury of the esophagus from acid reflux is very common.

Consider the following symptoms:

- Sharp or burning chest pain behind the breastbone (heartburn)

- Chest heaviness

- Regurgitation of partially digested or sour-tasting stomach contents

- Nausea

- Excessive saliva production

- Persistent cough

- Hoarse voice or lost voice

- A need to repeatedly clear your throat

- Asthma (wheezing)
